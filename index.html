Who am I?

I am currently working as a Research Associate at the Department of Biochemistry, University of Cambridge, UK. I am also an Adjunct Researcher to The Systems Biology Institute, Japan. At both these positions, I am engaged in cutting edge research in trans-omics data analytics, next-generation sequencing, de novo genome annotation, biological text mining, machine learning with a focus on developing and maintaining AI driven target discovery analytics pipelines in Python and R associated with the complex biological data. This includes, but not limited to, multi-omics, docking, network biology, machine/deep learning, etc. I am also working with several medical professionals helping them in precise diagnosis by providing efficient analytics for patient stratification and predictive biomarkers.

Before joining the current position, I was working as a full-time Research Scientist at The Systems Biology Institute, Japan. Here, I was responsible for developing and delivering AI-based solutions in pharma and non-pharma domain such as AI based toxicity predictor for a top cosmetic company, data-driven analytics for cancer sub- type identification, automated literature-based discovery (LBD) using text mining, etc. I was responsible for conducting high-quality research to ensure compliance with established industry standards, global regulatory guidelines and/or client contractual obligations. Provide asset management and solutions for complex client issues/problems. Collaborating with multiple teams to ensure timely review and release of reports to meet client requirements and deadlines. Based on my successful stint at this position, I have secured first-/co-authorships in multiple publications. I have also submitted a patent application for identifying new agents against human influenza virus.

As a detail-oriented and analytical professional with a PhD in Biological Information from the Tokyo Institute of technology, I have gained solid research experience in molecular biology, biochemistry and drug screening techniques including hands-on experience in structural biology with a focus on the development of multidimensional cheminformatics and toxicology assessment pipelines to efficiently screen chemicals on the toxicity and efficacy landscape. In my Ph.D., I discovered a novel protein target of anti-inflammatory drug salicylic acid the results of which were published in peer- reviewed articles and book chapters. My PhD acquainted me with the skills and newer concepts in various biochemical and biophysical techniques. Owing to the excellent findings in my Ph.D. work, I was offered an Assistant Professor position at Tokyo Medical University Hospital, Japan. Here, I was involved in Celgene funded multi- myeloma research. I was also the recipient of multiple public and private grants (DST- JSPS, Kobayashi International research grant) during this period.

I believe that I am intellectually prepared to participate, contribute and have my knowledge complemented by that of recognized scientists from different disciplines. I am confident that I will strive to succeed to the best of my ability.
